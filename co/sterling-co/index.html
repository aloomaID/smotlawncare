How often should I water my lawn, and what is the best time to do it?

Watering your lawn correctly is key to keeping it lush and green. At Smot Lawn Care, we often get asked, "How often should I water my lawn, and what's the best time to do it?" In Sterling Colorado the best practice is to water your lawn deeply 2-3 times a week. Overwatering can lead to shallow root growth and make your grass more susceptible to drought and diseases.
The best time to water your lawn is early in the morning, ideally between 6 a.m. and 10 a.m. This allows the grass to absorb moisture before the sun’s heat causes evaporation. Watering in the evening might seem convenient, but it can leave the lawn damp overnight, increasing the risk of fungal growth.
For optimal lawn health, use about 1 inch of water per session, which helps promote deeper root development. Adjust your watering schedule based on the weather; lawns in Sterling Colorado may need more frequent watering during hot, dry periods. Remember, it's not just about how often you water but how thoroughly you do it. Deep watering encourages roots to grow deeper, making your lawn more resilient.

How can I prevent weeds from overtaking my lawn?

Weeds can be a persistent challenge for homeowners, often overtaking a lawn and detracting from its health and beauty. At Smot Lawn Care, we understand that a weed-free lawn is crucial for both aesthetics and functionality. Here’s how you can prevent weeds from taking over your lawn in Sterling Colorado .
Firstly, maintaining a lush, healthy lawn is your best defense against weeds. Regular mowing at the correct height—typically 2.5 to 3 inches for most grass types—helps prevent weeds from getting enough sunlight to germinate. Additionally, ensuring your lawn receives adequate water and nutrients through proper fertilization strengthens your grass, making it more competitive against weed growth.
Secondly, applying a pre-emergent herbicide in early spring can prevent weed seeds from germinating. These herbicides create a barrier that stops weeds before they even have a chance to sprout. For ongoing weed management, consider applying a post-emergent herbicide to target and eliminate any existing weeds.
Mulching is another effective strategy, especially for garden beds adjacent to your lawn. A layer of mulch can smother weed seeds and prevent them from growing. Additionally, hand-pulling weeds regularly, especially before they flower and spread seeds, is a simple yet effective method to keep your lawn weed-free.
Finally, proper lawn aeration helps reduce soil compaction, which can contribute to weed problems. Aerating your lawn improves air and nutrient flow to the roots, promoting healthier grass growth.
